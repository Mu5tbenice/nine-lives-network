# Nerm VO — Recording Session Script

Printable script. Record in this order (grouped by tone), NOT shot order. 3–5 takes per line, varying tempos. Clean audio, no sync-to-picture yet — picture sync happens in CapCut.

**Voice anchors:** Ricky Gervais, Sam Hyde, Nick Mullen.

**Voice canon (from `server/services/nermBrain.js`):**
- lowercase always
- **no exclamation marks. ever.**
- short sentences, sometimes one word
- swears naturally when it fits — not every line
- never explain the joke
- mean from not caring, not from trying to hurt

Save takes to `audit/trailer/vo/takes/` with naming `s<shot>_<label>_take<N>.wav` — e.g. `s03_stormrage_take01.wav`.

## Block B — the house reveals (each with its own rhythm — see delivery notes)

**B1 — Shot 3 — ⚡ Stormrage**
> stormrage. big feelings. big storms. could use therapy. not available in nethara. tough.

*Delivery:* setup → pause → callback. "could use therapy" / [pause] / "not available in nethara" / [shorter pause] / "tough."

**B2 — Shot 4 — 🌿 Stonebark**
> stonebark. patient. ancient. one with the forest. hasn't moved in fourteen hundred years. probably asleep right now.

*Delivery:* shrug. "probably asleep right now" like you actually forgot why you were talking.

**B3 — Shot 5 — ☠️ Plaguemire**
> plaguemire. i love this one. smells like rotten lettuce and is genuinely just vibing.

*Delivery:* the ONLY line with warmth on the noun. Nerm is Plaguemire-affinity in canon. "i love this one" is real.

**B4 — Shot 6 — 💨 Ashenvale**
> ashenvale. fast. like, stupid fast. can't catch him. also can't hold a conversation. tradeoffs.

*Delivery:* tossed off. "tradeoffs" has zero commitment. Barely a word.

**B5 — Shot 7 — ☀️ Dawnbringer**
> dawnbringer. beautiful. pious. will heal anyone, no exceptions. including people you want dead. annoying.

*Delivery:* "annoying" is the punch. Punch-y but not angry.

**B6 — Shot 8 — 🔥 Smoulders**
> smoulders. has anger. has fire. has nothing else.

*Delivery:* the verdict. "has nothing else" hangs like a sentence with no lift on the last word.

## Block D — the monologue beats (rhythm matters)

**D1 — Shot 13 (ensemble money shot)**

## Session efficiency tips

- Do Block A first (easiest, warmup).
- Block B is the bulk — take a short break after each house.
- Block C last: these need the calm register, easier after you've settled in.
- Block D: requires the most range. Do when fully warmed up.
- Don't watch the picture yet. Eyes on the script, ears on your voice.
- If you stumble, say "line" and redo from the start of that line. Easy to cut in editing.

Expected session length: 45–60 minutes for all takes.

- Don't pick a single best take yet — leave options for the edit.
